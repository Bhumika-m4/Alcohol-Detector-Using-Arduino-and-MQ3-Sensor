# Literature Survey

Several researchers have explored alcohol detection technologies to improve road safety and workplace security.

Pranavan S Saravanan and colleagues discussed the working principles of MQ3 alcohol sensors and highlighted their applications in automotive, healthcare, and industrial environments. Their study emphasized the importance of sensor calibration and sensitivity in achieving accurate alcohol detection.

Smith's work on vehicle alcohol detection technologies examined advanced systems such as breath-based detectors and touch-based alcohol monitoring systems. The research demonstrated how integrating alcohol sensors into vehicles can significantly reduce drunk-driving incidents.

Dr. Pavan Shukla and co-authors proposed an automatic engine-locking mechanism that prevents vehicle ignition whenever alcohol is detected above a safe threshold. Their work focused on integrating MQ3 sensors with Arduino controllers to improve vehicle safety.

Walter Roberts and Sherry McKee investigated mobile alcohol biosensors and wearable technologies. Their study explored smartphone-based and portable alcohol monitoring systems capable of real-time tracking and healthcare integration.

These studies indicate that alcohol detection technology has significant potential in transportation, public safety, healthcare, and industrial monitoring systems.
